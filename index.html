Utopia in the Americas
Previous Next
Utopia in the Americas is a creative project/art book where I investigate & imagine economic rejuvenation in a public outdoor commercial center in Queretaro, Mexico. Through the reappropriation of space, I imagine what the commercial center could become if individuals dare to gather and engage in alternative practices. The art book is currently being sold in gallery Da Substanz and Libreria La Comenzon. This project highlights the importance of imagining the fruitful use of all spaces.
Derivas por China
Previous Next
Derivas por China was an urban investigation that I took with derivelab and was coordinated by Hannah Yu-Pearson. During the trip, we talked to various companies, firms, organizations and individuals on the innovations and practices that they were implementing in the city. While the experience was on foreign lands, I learned that talking to people, walking through the city, and familiarizing oneself with its spaces is imperative to acquiring real knowledge and information of a city, town and its people, regardless of where you are. The importance of outreach and meeting individuals and organizations was solidly established as one of my core principles during this trip. It is this prinicple that I apply to all of my products.

Geodesign Grinnell
Previous Next
During this project, I interviewed several farmers in Grinnell, Iowa to inquire on their thoughts on climate change and big corporate agriculture, which has rapidly been putting farmers out of businness all across America, and ask how they plan to tackle these problems, if at all. Through the process, I realized how disconnected they were from one another, often sharing similar ideas, opinions and solutions to complex problems but hesitant to communicate with others. Despite their proximity, they were not truly united to help each other when and if these problems worsen. My products are tools that will help individuals collaborate, chat, negotiate and truly unite and connect for a common cause.
LocisNet
Previous Next
LocisNet is a new type of social platform that was inspired by noticing the state of technological culture in present society and by researching its adverse effects. LocisNet is a new type of tool that aims at fostering the growth of local human networks, offline communication, and local information networks as opposed to global networks, online communication, and media-based information. It is also an anonymous platform that rids itself of identity based connection and replaces that with idea based connections and place identity. Coming Soon!

About Me

I am a full-stack software developer, designer, community-based researcher, art enthusiast, sustainability advocate and engaged citizen. I am passionate about using my skill-set to create technological solutions to complex problems.

My specialty is in creating applications and websites that help individuals engage more fruitfully within their communities. I aim to help expand the local networks of individuals, to improve the rates of offline communication and interaction in society, to change the way information and news is distributed within society, and to help local economies grow.

The tools that I create are based on human-centered research and design. I hope to continue to solve difficult and unseen challenges through human-centered design and hands-on communication with clients, organizations and individuals.
